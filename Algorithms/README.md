## Asymptotic Analysis of Algorithms

Usually, the time required by an algorithm comes under three types:

1. **Worst Case**:
- It takes **a huge amount of time** for the program execution.

2. **Average Case**:
- It takes **an average amount of time** for the program execution.

3. **Best case**:
- It takes **lowest amount of time** for the program execution.

### Asymptotic Notations

Asymptotic Notation is a mathematical notation used to describe the asymptotic behaviour of a function.

These notations are used for calculating the run-time complexity of an algorithm.

#### Big O Notation

- The formal way of expressing the upper boundary of an algorithm's run-time.
- It measures the worst-case time complexity.

#### Omega Notation

- The formal way of expressing the lower boundary of an algorithm's run-time.
- It measures the best-case time complexity.

#### Theta Notation

- The formal way of expressing both the upper and lower boundaries of an algorithm's run-time.
- It measures the best-case and worst-case time complexity
